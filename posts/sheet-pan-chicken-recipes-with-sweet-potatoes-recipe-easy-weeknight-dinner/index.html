Sheet Pan Chicken Recipes With Sweet Potatoes Recipe: Easy Weeknight Dinner

Sarah Mitchell
Published on April 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
35 min Time
4 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This sheet pan chicken with sweet potatoes is a simple, flavorful dinner that comes together effortlessly. Roasting everything on one pan means less cleanup and a perfect balance of tender chicken and caramelized sweet potatoes. Great for busy weeknights!

Pro Tips

Pat the chicken dry before seasoning and roasting.
Moisture on the chicken skin creates steam during cooking, which prevents browning and crispiness. Patting it dry removes excess water, allowing the heat to create a crispy, golden skin through the Maillard reaction.
Give the sweet potatoes a 10-minute head start in the oven.
Sweet potatoes take longer to cook than chicken thighs. Starting them first ensures they become tender and caramelized without overcooking the chicken, which could dry out if roasted too long.
Use a meat thermometer to check doneness.
Visual cues can be misleading, especially with bone-in chicken. A thermometer ensures the chicken reaches 165°F (74°C), guaranteeing it’s fully cooked and safe to eat while avoiding dryness.
Turn sweet potatoes halfway through roasting.
Turning prevents uneven cooking and promotes caramelization on all sides. This step avoids soggy spots and enhances the sweet potatoes’ texture and flavor.
Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet.
Crowding traps steam and prevents browning. Spreading ingredients out allows hot air to circulate, producing crisp skin and caramelized potatoes.
Rest the chicken uncovered for 5 minutes after roasting.
Resting lets juices redistribute within the meat, making it juicy. Leaving it uncovered keeps the skin crisp by preventing steam buildup.
Use bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs for best flavor and texture.
The bone adds flavor and helps keep the meat moist, while the skin crisps up beautifully in the oven, enhancing both taste and mouthfeel.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping the step to pat chicken dry

Why it happens Many cooks overlook this because it feels like an extra step or they don’t realize moisture affects crispiness.

Fix Always use paper towels to thoroughly dry the chicken skin before seasoning and roasting. This prevents steaming and ensures the skin crisps up nicely.

Adding chicken and sweet potatoes to the pan at the same time

Why it happens It’s tempting to simplify by roasting everything together from the start.

Fix Roast sweet potatoes alone for 10 minutes first, then add the chicken. This prevents undercooked potatoes or overcooked chicken.

Overcrowding the pan

Why it happens Trying to fit all ingredients on one pan without spacing.

Fix Use a large enough sheet pan and spread ingredients out in a single layer to allow proper air circulation for browning.

Not using a thermometer to check chicken doneness

Why it happens Relying on guesswork or appearance alone.

Fix Invest in a meat thermometer and check for 165°F (74°C) internal temperature to ensure safe, juicy chicken.

Covering chicken immediately after roasting

Why it happens Thinking it will keep the chicken warm or moist.

Fix Let chicken rest uncovered for 5 minutes to keep skin crispy and juices sealed inside.

Variations

Spicy Sheet Pan Chicken and Sweet Potatoes

Add a teaspoon of cayenne pepper or chili powder to the seasoning mix for a spicy kick. You can also toss sliced jalapeños on the pan for extra heat. Expect a bolder, warming flavor profile that pairs well with a cooling yogurt sauce.

Note: Spicy seasonings don’t affect cooking time but watch for spice levels to suit your taste.

Herb-Infused Sheet Pan Chicken with Sweet Potatoes

Swap dried thyme for fresh rosemary and add fresh garlic cloves to the pan. This variation offers a fragrant, earthy aroma and deeper herbal notes that elevate the dish’s complexity.

Note: Fresh herbs added at the start roast well but can burn if left too long; consider adding some halfway through cooking.

Sheet Pan Chicken with Sweet Potatoes and Brussels Sprouts

Add halved Brussels sprouts to the sheet pan for extra veggies. They roast beautifully alongside sweet potatoes and chicken, adding a slightly bitter contrast that balances the sweetness.

Note: Brussels sprouts may need a slight timing adjustment—add them with the sweet potatoes for even cooking.

Honey Mustard Glazed Sheet Pan Chicken and Sweet Potatoes

Brush the chicken with a mix of honey and Dijon mustard before roasting. This glaze caramelizes in the oven, adding a sweet and tangy dimension that pairs perfectly with the natural sweetness of the potatoes.

Note: Apply glaze after the first 10 minutes of roasting to prevent burning the sugars.

Sheet Pan Chicken with Sweet Potatoes and Apples

Add peeled and sliced tart apples to the pan for a sweet and savory twist. The apples soften and caramelize, complementing the chicken and sweet potatoes with a fresh fruity note.

Note: Add apples with the sweet potatoes to ensure they cook through without becoming mushy.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

This dish can safely sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours. Cover loosely with foil or a clean kitchen towel to keep dust and insects away.

Refrigerator

Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. The chicken skin may soften when chilled, so reheat in the oven to restore crispness. Sweet potatoes reheat well but may dry out slightly.

Freezer

Freeze cooked chicken and sweet potatoes in a freezer-safe container or bag for up to 3 months. Wrap tightly to prevent freezer burn. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator before reheating.

Reheating

Reheat in a 375°F (190°C) oven for 15-20 minutes until warmed through and skin is crispy again. Avoid microwaving as it tends to make the skin soggy. Add a splash of olive oil if needed to refresh texture.

Make Ahead

You can prep and season the sweet potatoes and chicken up to 24 hours in advance, storing them separately in the fridge. Assemble on the sheet pan and roast when ready. This saves time on busy days without sacrificing flavor.

FAQ

Can I use boneless chicken thighs for this recipe?
Yes, but boneless thighs will cook faster and won’t have the same crispy skin or juicy texture as bone-in, skin-on thighs. Reduce cooking time by about 5-10 minutes and watch closely to avoid drying out.
What can I substitute for sweet potatoes?
You can use regular potatoes, but they may need a slightly longer roasting time. Other root vegetables like carrots or butternut squash also work well and add different flavors and textures.
How do I make the chicken skin extra crispy?
Pat the skin dry thoroughly before seasoning, roast at a high temperature (425°F), and avoid covering the chicken after cooking. Also, don’t overcrowd the pan, which traps steam and softens skin.
Can I add other vegetables to the sheet pan?
Absolutely! Vegetables like Brussels sprouts, bell peppers, or onions roast well alongside chicken and sweet potatoes. Just ensure they are cut to similar sizes for even cooking.
Is it okay to marinate the chicken overnight?
Yes, marinating can add flavor, but be careful with acidic marinades as they can change the texture if left too long. For this recipe, a simple dry rub is usually enough.
Can I make this recipe gluten-free?
Yes, this recipe is naturally gluten-free as long as you use gluten-free spices and avoid any sauces with gluten-containing ingredients.
How do I know when the sweet potatoes are done?
They should be tender enough to pierce easily with a fork and have a caramelized, slightly crispy exterior. If they’re still hard in the middle, roast longer.
Can I use frozen sweet potatoes?
Frozen sweet potatoes can be used but may release more moisture, which can affect roasting. Pat them dry well and consider lowering the oven temperature slightly to avoid sogginess.
What’s the best way to store leftovers?
Store cooled leftovers in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven to restore texture rather than microwaving.
Can I double this recipe?
Yes, but use two sheet pans or a very large pan to avoid overcrowding. Cooking times may vary slightly, so check doneness carefully.

What You'll Need

4 servings

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 2 large sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

How To Do It

Step 1
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). This high temperature is key to getting that crispy skin on the chicken while roasting the sweet potatoes to a golden, tender finish. Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup. Toss the cubed sweet potatoes with half the olive oil, half the smoked paprika, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Spread them out in an even layer on the baking sheet. Starting the sweet potatoes first allows them to cook through without drying out by the time the chicken is added.
⏱ Time: 10 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sweet potatoes should start to soften and develop a slight golden color around the edges.
Step 2
While the sweet potatoes roast, pat the chicken thighs dry with paper towels—this helps the skin crisp up nicely. Rub the chicken with the remaining olive oil and season generously with smoked paprika, garlic powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. After the sweet potatoes have roasted for 10 minutes, remove the pan from the oven and nestle the chicken thighs skin-side up among the potatoes. Return the pan to the oven. The hot oven will immediately start crisping the chicken skin while finishing the cooking process evenly.
⏱ Time: 25 minutes
✓ Doneness: Chicken skin should be golden brown and crispy; juices should run clear when pierced.
Step 3
About halfway through roasting, use tongs to gently turn the sweet potatoes to ensure they cook evenly and get a bit of caramelization on all sides. Resist the urge to move the chicken around; keeping it skin-side up helps maintain crispness. This step is important to avoid soggy potatoes and to get that nice roasted texture.
⏱ Time: 12 minutes
✓ Doneness: Sweet potatoes are tender on the inside and caramelized on the outside.
Step 4
Once the chicken reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) and the sweet potatoes are fork-tender, remove the pan from the oven. Let the chicken rest for a few minutes before serving; this helps the juices redistribute and keeps the meat juicy. Sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the top for a fresh pop of color and flavor.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes rest
✓ Doneness: Chicken is juicy and tender; sweet potatoes are soft and flavorful.
Step 5
Serve the chicken thighs alongside the roasted sweet potatoes straight from the pan for a rustic, comforting meal. You can add a simple green salad or steamed veggies if you want to round out the plate. This dish pairs well with a squeeze of lemon or a drizzle of hot sauce if you like a bit of brightness or heat.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Ready to enjoy!
Step 6
If you want to prep ahead, you can cut and season the sweet potatoes the night before and store them in the fridge. Just toss them with oil and spices, cover, and refrigerate. When ready to cook, add them to the pan and roast as directed. This makes weeknight dinners even quicker.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes prep
✓ Doneness: Sweet potatoes seasoned and ready for roasting.
Step 7
For extra flavor, try adding sliced onions or bell peppers to the pan with the sweet potatoes. They roast beautifully and add a nice sweetness and texture contrast. Just be sure not to overcrowd the pan to maintain that crisp roasting effect.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Vegetables are tender and caramelized.
Step 8
Use a meat thermometer to check the chicken’s internal temperature. This is the most reliable way to ensure it’s fully cooked without drying out. Insert the thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh without touching bone. When it hits 165°F (74°C), it’s safe to eat.
⏱ Time: 0 minutes
✓ Doneness: Thermometer reads 165°F (74°C).
Step 9
Let the chicken rest uncovered on the pan for 5 minutes after removing from the oven to keep the skin crispy. Covering it immediately traps steam and can make the skin soggy. This small step really helps keep the texture perfect.
⏱ Time: 5 minutes
✓ Doneness: Skin remains crisp and chicken stays juicy.
