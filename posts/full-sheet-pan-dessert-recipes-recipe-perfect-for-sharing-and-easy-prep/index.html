Full Sheet Pan Dessert Recipes Recipe: Perfect for Sharing and Easy Prep

Sarah Mitchell
Published on April 28, 2026
15 min Prep Time
30 min Time
12 Servings
Easy Difficulty

This full sheet pan dessert recipe is a game changer when you want something sweet, simple, and shareable. It uses a rich buttery crust topped with gooey chocolate and nuts, baked all at once. Great for parties or family nights!

Pro Tips

Chill the dough before baking the crust.
Chilling firms the butter, preventing the crust from shrinking or becoming tough during baking. It also helps the dough relax, reducing cracks and ensuring an even texture.
Partially bake the crust before adding the topping.
This step sets the base so the topping doesn’t soak in and make it soggy. It ensures a crisp, tender crust that holds up well under the moist topping.
Use a sharp knife and wipe between cuts.
A sharp knife creates clean edges and prevents the topping from dragging or crumbling. Wiping between cuts keeps slices neat and visually appealing.
Don’t overmix the dough or topping.
Overmixing develops gluten in the flour, making the crust tough. It also can cause the topping to become dense instead of chewy and tender.
Evenly distribute chocolate chips and nuts.
Even layering ensures every bite has balanced flavor and texture. Clumping can cause uneven baking or overly sweet spots.
Cool completely before slicing.
Cooling firms the dessert, making it easier to cut without crumbling and allowing flavors to meld for a better taste experience.
Use room temperature eggs for the topping.
Room temperature eggs mix more evenly into the sugar, producing a smooth, consistent topping that bakes uniformly.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping chilling the dough.

Why it happens Many rush to bake immediately, thinking it saves time, not realizing the impact on texture.

Fix Always chill the dough for at least 15 minutes. This solidifies the butter and helps the crust maintain its shape and tenderness during baking.

Not prebaking the crust.

Why it happens Some assume everything can bake together in one step to save effort.

Fix Partially bake the crust first to set it, preventing sogginess. This step is essential for a crisp base under the moist topping.

Overbaking the dessert.

Why it happens Fear of raw dough leads to baking too long, drying out the topping and nuts.

Fix Watch carefully in the last minutes. The topping should be set but still glossy and chewy, not dry or cracked.

Using melted or warm butter in the crust.

Why it happens Some think melted butter is easier to mix.

Fix Use cold butter cut into the flour. This creates flaky layers and a tender crust. Melted butter results in a dense, greasy base.

Cutting the dessert while still warm.

Why it happens Impatience to taste leads to messy slices.

Fix Cool completely to let the topping set. If in a hurry, chill briefly in the fridge before slicing for cleaner cuts.

Variations

Nut-Free Version

Simply omit the nuts or replace them with toasted pumpkin or sunflower seeds for crunch. This keeps the texture interesting without allergy concerns. Expect a slightly less toasty flavor but still delicious.

Note: Seeds toast faster than nuts, so add them halfway through baking to avoid burning.

Add Fresh Berries

Scatter fresh raspberries or blueberries over the chocolate chips before adding the topping for a fruity twist. The berries add tartness that balances the sweet, rich layers.

Note: Use firm berries and gently press them into the crust to prevent sinking. Bake immediately to avoid sogginess.

Salted Caramel Drizzle

Once cooled, drizzle homemade or store-bought salted caramel sauce over the bars for extra indulgence. The salty-sweet combo enhances the chocolate and nut flavors beautifully.

Note: Add drizzle just before serving to keep the topping from becoming soggy.

Peanut Butter Swirl

Drop dollops of peanut butter on top of the chocolate chips and swirl gently with a knife before adding the topping. This adds a creamy, nutty layer that pairs perfectly with chocolate.

Note: Use smooth peanut butter for easy swirling and even distribution.

Coconut Topping

Sprinkle shredded unsweetened coconut over the nuts before baking for a tropical flair. The coconut toasts nicely and adds a chewy texture contrast.

Note: Watch the coconut closely during baking as it can brown quickly.

Storage & Tips

Room Temperature

Keep covered at room temperature for up to 2 days. Use a cake dome or wrap tightly with plastic wrap to prevent drying out. The dessert stays moist but avoid leaving it out longer to prevent spoilage.

Refrigerator

Store in an airtight container for up to 5 days. The dessert firms up and can be enjoyed cold or brought to room temperature before serving. Refrigeration may slightly harden the chocolate topping.

Freezer

Freeze wrapped tightly in plastic wrap and foil for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator, then bring to room temperature before serving to restore texture.

Reheating

Warm slices gently in a microwave for 10-15 seconds or in a low oven (300°F) for 5-7 minutes. Add a small splash of milk if the topping feels too firm. Avoid overheating to keep the gooey texture intact.

Make Ahead

Prepare the crust and topping separately a day ahead. Store the crust dough chilled and topping mixture refrigerated. Assemble and bake on the day of serving for the freshest texture and flavor.

FAQ

Can I use a different type of chocolate instead of semi-sweet chips?
Absolutely! You can swap semi-sweet chips for milk, dark, or white chocolate depending on your preference. Just keep in mind that milk chocolate melts faster and sweeter, while dark chocolate offers a richer, slightly bitter contrast. Avoid using baking chocolate bars unless chopped finely, as they have no sugar and a different melting profile.
What if my crust shrinks during baking?
Crust shrinkage usually happens when the dough isn’t chilled enough or is overworked. Make sure to chill the dough before baking and avoid stretching it when pressing into the pan. Also, avoid using too warm butter or overmixing, which develops gluten and causes toughness.
Can I make this dessert gluten-free?
Yes! Substitute the all-purpose flour with a gluten-free baking blend that includes xanthan gum for structure. The texture might be slightly different—less flaky and more crumbly—but still delicious. Make sure your other ingredients are gluten-free certified to avoid cross-contamination.
How do I store leftovers to keep them fresh?
Store leftovers in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days or refrigerate for up to 5 days. Cover tightly to prevent drying out. If refrigerated, bring to room temperature or warm slightly before serving for best texture.
Can I double or halve this recipe?
You can double the recipe if you have a larger sheet pan, but baking times may vary slightly—watch closely to avoid overbaking. Halving works well for smaller pans; just adjust baking time accordingly. Use an 8x8 inch pan for half the recipe and reduce bake time by about 5-7 minutes.
What causes the topping to be too runny or not set?
If the topping doesn’t set, it usually means the eggs weren’t cooked through or the baking time was too short. Make sure to bake until the topping is golden and slightly glossy. Also, don’t overwhisk the eggs or add too much liquid, which can prevent proper setting.
Is it okay to use salted butter?
You can use salted butter, but reduce or omit additional salt in the crust to avoid oversalting. Unsalted butter is preferred because it gives you more control over seasoning and flavor balance.
How do I prevent nuts from burning?
Nuts toast quickly, so add them evenly and watch the dessert closely during the final bake. If you notice browning too soon, tent the pan with foil to protect the nuts while the topping finishes baking.

What You'll Need

Approximately 12 large servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts or pecans
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

How To Do It

Step 1
Start by making the crust. Combine the sifted flour, granulated sugar, and salt in a large bowl. Cut in the cold, cubed butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. This step is crucial because those little butter bits create a flaky texture when baked. Avoid overworking the dough, or it will become tough. Once crumbly, add the vanilla extract and mix just until it comes together. Press this dough evenly into a greased full sheet pan, making sure to cover all corners. Chilling the crust in the fridge for 15 minutes before baking helps prevent it from shrinking and keeps the texture tender.
⏱ Time: 20
✓ Doneness: The crust should look evenly pressed with no cracks and feel firm to the touch after chilling.
Step 2
Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the chilled crust for 15 minutes until it’s just starting to turn golden around the edges. This partial bake firms it up so the topping doesn’t soak in and make it soggy. If you skip this step, the dessert might be too soft or undercooked in the center. Keep an eye on the crust’s color; you want a light golden hue, not dark brown. Once baked, remove it from the oven and let it cool slightly while you prepare the topping.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Edges should be lightly golden and the crust should feel set and not doughy.
Step 3
While the crust bakes, whisk together the brown sugar, eggs, and baking powder in a medium bowl until smooth. This mixture will create a rich, caramel-like topping that sets beautifully over the crust. The eggs act as a binder, and the baking powder adds a subtle lift. If you rush this step or don’t whisk well, the topping might be uneven or too dense. Take your time to get a smooth, glossy batter.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: The mixture should be smooth, slightly thick, and uniform in color.
Step 4
Sprinkle the semi-sweet chocolate chips evenly over the partially baked crust. This layering is key because the warm crust will help the chips soften and melt slightly, creating that gooey texture everyone loves. Don’t skip this step or mix the chips into the batter; letting them sit on top ensures they melt just right without sinking. You can also gently press them into the crust for better adhesion.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Chocolate chips should be evenly distributed and slightly embedded in the crust.
Step 5
Pour the brown sugar and egg topping over the chocolate chips in an even layer. Use a spatula to spread it gently, covering all chips but not mixing them in. This topping will bake into a glossy, chewy layer that contrasts nicely with the crisp crust. Be careful not to over-spread or the topping might be too thin in spots and dry out. The even layer ensures consistent texture and flavor in every bite.
⏱ Time: 5
✓ Doneness: Topping should cover the chocolate chips fully and look smooth and shiny.
Step 6
Sprinkle the chopped walnuts or pecans evenly over the topping. Nuts add a wonderful crunch and a toasty flavor that balances the sweetness. Make sure to distribute them evenly for consistent bites. If you want a nut-free version, you can skip this or substitute with seeds like pumpkin or sunflower. Toasting the nuts lightly beforehand intensifies their flavor but is optional.
⏱ Time: 3
✓ Doneness: Nuts should cover the surface evenly and look fresh and slightly glossy.
Step 7
Return the pan to the oven and bake for another 15 minutes, or until the topping is set and golden brown. Keep an eye on it in the last few minutes to avoid burning the nuts or edges. The topping should look firm but still slightly glossy, indicating it’s perfectly baked. If you bake too long, it can dry out and lose that chewy texture that makes this dessert special.
⏱ Time: 15
✓ Doneness: Topping should be golden with a slight sheen and nuts toasted but not burnt.
Step 8
Once baked, remove the sheet pan from the oven and let it cool completely on a wire rack. Cooling is essential because the dessert firms up as it cools, making it easier to cut clean squares without crumbling. If you try to slice it warm, the topping might be too soft and messy. Patience here pays off with neat, beautiful pieces.
⏱ Time: 30
✓ Doneness: Dessert should be firm to the touch and no longer warm in the center.
Step 9
Use a sharp knife to cut the dessert into squares or rectangles. Wiping the knife clean between cuts helps keep edges neat. Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled. This dessert pairs wonderfully with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a drizzle of caramel sauce for extra indulgence. Store leftovers in an airtight container.
⏱ Time: 10
✓ Doneness: Squares should hold their shape with clean edges and no crumbling.
